The 3-Day Strength Build

Quick, easy, simple. Three days a week, four big lifts a day, and one job: get stronger. Built on Greg O'Gallagher's Kinobody principles — Upper / Lower / Upper, Reverse Pyramid Training, and progression you can actually track.

📅 3 days/week 🔺 RPT 6 · 8 · 10 ⏱️ ~45–60 min/session 📈 Double progression

01The week at a glance

Train three days on non-consecutive days so each session lands fresh. The classic is Monday / Wednesday / Friday — but Tue/Thu/Sat works just as well. The two upper days are different (A and B) so nothing gets stale and push/pull stay balanced across the week.

02How to run every set

Reverse Pyramid Training (RPT) is the engine of this program. You hit your heaviest set first, while you're freshest and strongest, then drop the weight ~10% each following set so you can keep the reps high. It's the opposite of the usual "warm up into your top set" — and it's why you get strong without living in the gym.

3 working sets

Per main lift, after warm-ups. Reps climb each set: 6 → 8 → 10.

Drop ~10% each set

Heaviest set 1. Take roughly 10% off the bar for set 2, another 10% for set 3.

Rest 3 minutes

Between work sets on the big lifts. Long rest = full power on every set. ~90s on finishers.

Leave 1 rep in the tank

Stop ~1 rep short of failure on set 1. Sets 2–3 can be taken close to the limit.

Here's exactly what it looks like for someone whose top incline-press set is 80 lb dumbbells:

SetTarget repsWeightRest after
Set 16 reps80 lb (heaviest)3 min
Set 28 reps72 lb (−10%)3 min
Set 310 reps65 lb (−10%)done
Warm up first. Before set 1 of your first heavy lift, do 2–3 quick ramp-up sets (e.g. 50% × 5, 70% × 3, 85% × 1) — light, no fatigue. These don't count as working sets. Later lifts in the session need only one easy primer set.

03The program

Run your RPT 6 / 8 / 10 on the heavy compound lifts. The finishers (curls, pushdowns, raises, abs) use lighter, higher-rep "straight sets" — same weight-ish, just chase a good pump and clean form.

Why two different upper days? Upper A leads with pressing so you're fresh for push strength; Upper B leads with rowing and adds dips + lateral raises. Over the week every movement pattern gets trained ~twice, from a slightly different angle — more balanced shoulders and back, zero boredom.

04The 5 lifts that matter

You don't need to track everything. Kinobody's idea is to pick a handful of "indicator lifts" and relentlessly drive them up. Get these stronger and the physique follows. Log them every session:

1. Incline DB Press

Upper chest + front delts. Your main pressing number.

2. Weighted Chin-up

Back width + biceps. Strength-to-weight king.

3. Overhead Press

Shoulders + triceps. The hardest to add weight to — be patient.

4. Back Squat

Whole lower body + core. Your one big leg number.

5. Romanian Deadlift

Hamstrings + glutes + posterior chain.

(Swap-in) Weighted Dip

Sub for incline press on Upper B if you prefer dips.

05How you actually get stronger

The whole program runs on double progression. Each lift has a rep target for set 1 (6 reps). You don't add weight on a feeling — you add it when you earn it:

This weekSet 1 resultNext week
Incline press @ 80 lbYou get 6 reps (the target)Go up: 82.5 lb, aim for 6 again
Incline press @ 80 lbYou only get 4–5 repsStay at 80 lb, build to 6
Incline press @ 80 lbYou smash 7–8 repsJump up: +5 lb next time

Small jumps win

Upper body: +2.5–5 lb. Lower body: +5–10 lb. Micro-loading beats ego-loading every time.

One number to chase

Drive set 1. When it goes up, sets 2 and 3 (your −10% / −20% sets) follow automatically.

06Rules, swaps & common questions

How long should this take?

45–60 minutes. The 3-minute rests feel long, but that's the point — they're what let you lift heavy on every set. Bring something to do between sets.

Should I deload?

Yes. Every 6–8 weeks, take one lighter week — same lifts, ~60% of your weights, stop well short of hard. You'll come back stronger.

What if I can't do weighted chin-ups yet?

Start with bodyweight chin-ups for reps. Once you can do 10+ clean, start adding weight with a belt or a dumbbell between your feet. Use an assisted machine or bands if you can't hit a few yet.

Can I add an arm/ab day?

Keep it 3 days while you're getting stronger — that's the whole appeal. If you're recovering well and want more, add a short optional 4th "pump" day of arms, lateral raises and abs. Never make it heavy.

Nutrition, briefly

Strength needs fuel and protein. Eat ~0.8–1 g of protein per lb of bodyweight daily. Slight calorie surplus to build, slight deficit to lean out — but keep the weights going up either way.

And the calves?

Per Anthony: skipped. 😎 If you ever want them, tack on 3 sets of standing calf raises at the end of Lower day. No judgment.
