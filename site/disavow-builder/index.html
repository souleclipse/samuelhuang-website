Disavow Builder

Turn a list of toxic referring domains into a Google-ready disavow.txt. Pick a saved domain from the dropdown to load its pre-built spam set, or paste your own domains or an Ahrefs referring-domains CSV export. It strips protocols and paths, keeps the root domain, dedupes, and writes one domain: directive per line in the format Search Console expects.

Read before you upload. Disavowing is a blunt, hard-to-reverse tool. It tells Google to ignore links from these domains permanently. Only disavow domains you are confident are spam or part of a negative-SEO attack. A wrong entry throws away a real link. Google also says most sites never need this file, its spam systems already ignore obvious junk. Review every line.

How to use it

  1. In Ahrefs Site Explorer → Referring domains, filter to the junk (DR/spam flag), export to CSV, paste it on the left. Or paste a plain domain list.
  2. Click Build, then review every line. Remove anything that is actually a legitimate link.
  3. Download the file and upload it at the Google Disavow Links tool for the property. One file per property, and a new upload replaces the old one, so keep the full list, not just additions.
  4. Effect is slow. Google reprocesses links on its own recrawl schedule, expect weeks, not days.
